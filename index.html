01 Welcome

A Word of Welcome

From the desk of our leadership — an invitation to come hungry, stay surrendered, and go deeper than ever before.

SP

Rev. Sam Ponusamy

General Superintendent · UPC Malaysia

Greetings! On behalf of our UPC leadership team, it is a profound joy to welcome all of you to our annual camp meeting 2026. It is wonderful to see familiar faces and to extend a special welcome to those joining us for the first time. In line with this year’s theme, “Go Deeper,” let us press past the shallows and seek God with all of our hearts, prioritising His presence and His knowledge above all else.

“You will seek Me and find Me, when you seek Me with all your heart.” — Jeremiah 29:13

We recognise and appreciate that many have made much sacrifice — organising, fasting and praying — to gather for this special time of fellowship and spiritual renewal. This gathering is more than a social event; it is an opportunity to deepen our relationship with God. I encourage you to be open to the messages God has for you, and may you leave this place with a renewed dedication and a fresh outpouring of His Spirit.

JC

Rev. Joel Charles

District Superintendent · UPCWM

Peace and grace to all! We count it a joy and privilege to gather once again from different churches to worship King Jesus in Spirit and in Truth, here in Kampar. Taking time off from the busyness of our daily schedules offers us a moment to pause, reflect and receive fresh lessons from the Lord.

We live in a time when many voices vie for our attention. More than ever, we must stay grounded in God’s Word — prayerful, humble, teachable and unoffendable. A meeting such as this gives us the opportunity to recalibrate our walk and sharpen our focus. We thank Rev. Joel Bean and Rev. Lonnie Vestal for taking the time to minister the Word. I pray this will be a life-changing experience as we answer the call to go deeper.

Guest Speaker

Rev. Lonnie Vestal

Guest Speaker · A Word of Welcome

Dear Brothers and Sisters, we come once again to this wonderful time of spiritual refreshing. The theme for this year’s Called to Greatness is “Deeper.” The Apostle Paul greatly desired that his saints would deepen their knowledge of Jesus Christ when he wrote:

“That Christ may dwell in your hearts by faith; that ye, being rooted and grounded in love, may be able to comprehend with all saints what is the breadth, and length, and depth, and height; and to know the love of Christ, which passeth knowledge, that ye might be filled with all the fulness of God.” — Ephesians 3:17–19

In just two verses, Paul describes close fellowship with Jesus four different ways: “Christ dwelling in your heart,” “being rooted and grounded in love,” “knowing the love of Christ,” and “filled with all the fulness of God.” If Paul were to condense his instructions into one statement, he might have simply said, “go deeper.”

This week, we desire to go deeper into the things of the Spirit, and Jesus beckons us, invites us to a deeper walk with Him, a deeper knowledge of His love, a deeper understanding of His will for our lives.

Brothers and sisters, this is a week of visitation! Let us be renewed with power from on high. Let us be strengthened in His love, and let us go deeper in Him than ever before.

Yours in Christ,
Rev. Lonnie Vestal

04 For a Blessed Camp

Rules & Regulations

From the Camp Committee

Please observe
1Safety
  • The campground is large — keep young children under supervision, especially during recreational sessions.
  • No lifeguard is provided at the swimming pool; parents must remain vigilant.
  • Children may swim only under parental supervision.
2Hygiene
  • Kindly keep the meeting halls and break-out rooms clean for everyone’s comfort.
  • The dining area is a common space — please be mindful of cleanliness.
3Participation
  • All participants are highly encouraged to join every session — prayer, morning & evening services, meals, and recreation — for bonding and fellowship.
4Meals
  • Breakfast (Fri–Sun): 8:00 – 9:00 AM
  • Dinner (Thu–Sat): 6:00 – 7:00 PM
  • Lunch (Fri–Sun): 12:30 – 1:30 PM

From Grand Kampar Hotel

Hotel policy

1Campers are NOT to take the mattress down from the bed and sleep on the divan. Penalty is RM70. If the divan is broken there will be a charge of RM400.00 per divan.

2The hotel will consider non-attendance only in special cases, such as hospitalisation or the passing of an immediate family member.

3Meals are buffet, with replenishment only during the first hour.

4Check-in is at 3:00 PM on the first day of camp; check-out is at 12:00 PM on the last day. Each room is given two key cards — lost key cards will be charged RM20 per card.

5Any destruction or dirtying of walls, carpets in the seminar halls, or other hotel property will incur a minimum charge of RM300.00 from Housekeeping for cleaning/repainting.

6Please ensure all food brought into the seminar hall is halal. Non-halal food may be brought into campers’ own rooms. No durians, please.

7Each hotel room is equipped with a fridge and a hair dryer.

8An iron and ironing board are available in the corridor outside the hotel rooms for campers to use.
